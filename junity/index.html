Franchise Economy

JUNITY franchise is a profitable business without large investments

Here is an example of economics of our schools in Los Angeles (USA):

Basic data

The franchise fee for this location is $12 000

Recommended membership price is 240 USD per month for 8 training sessions, at the cost of 30 USD / session

Expected attendance per training session: 30 children

Income per lesson

30 children х 30 USD = 900 USD

Expenses

1. Field rental for a 1-hour class is 75 USD

2. Coach salary

Each coach or assistant coach can train up to 8 players. 30 players require 2 coaches and 2 coach assistants. As an example, a coach gets paid 50 USD and assistant coach - 35 USD per lesson, for a total of 170 USD

Total outlays/training session: 245 USD

Profit

Gross profit from one training session: 900 - 245 = 655 USD

8 training sessions per month, gross profit per month is 5 240 USD

Minus advertising expenses averaging 15 USD / child х 30 children = 450 USD

Minus monthly fee of 500 USD

Net profit per month is = 4 290 USD

At the end of the third month total net profit will be equal to 12 870 USD, which covers the initial fee of 12 000 USD and the school begins to produce a net income flow

After reaching the breakeven point of the profit of each school is expected to be at 4 290 USD per month (for one group of 30 children / 8 working hours)
